About Attorney Glik

Simon Glik — A Fighter in the Courtroom & Beyond

Simon Glik is a Boston-based attorney with over two decades of experience representing immigrants, the accused, and those whose civil rights have been violated by the state. A graduate of Boston University School of Law, he has built his practice on the belief that the legal system must remain equally accessible to every person — regardless of wealth, immigration status, or political power. His firm, headquartered in Allston, serves clients across Greater Boston and Massachusetts, with bilingual services in English and Spanish to ensure that language is never a barrier to justice.

Attorney Glik is known throughout the Boston legal community for his meticulous case preparation, his willingness to take difficult cases to trial, and his deep commitment to each client's outcome. He brings the same personal investment to a first-time misdemeanor as he does to a complex deportation defense — because he understands that every case represents someone's life, family, or freedom. He is an active member of the Massachusetts Bar Association, the National Immigration Project, and the National Lawyers Guild.

⚖️
Landmark Case: Glik v. Cunniffe (1st Cir. 2011)
In 2011, the U.S. Court of Appeals for the First Circuit ruled in Attorney Glik's favor, establishing that citizens have a clearly established First Amendment right to record police officers carrying out their duties in public spaces. The case arose when Boston police arrested Glik for filming an arrest on the Boston Common. The precedent set by this decision has been cited in courts across the country and continues to protect civil liberties nationwide.

Stay calm, remain silent, and ask for a lawyer immediately. You have the right to remain silent under the Fifth Amendment — and you should use it. Do not answer questions, explain yourself, or consent to searches. Politely but clearly state: "I am invoking my right to remain silent and I want to speak with an attorney." Then stop talking. Police officers are trained to elicit incriminating information; anything you say can and will be used against you. Contact Attorney Glik as soon as you're able — the earlier we become involved, the more options we have to protect you. Lawful permanent residents can be placed in removal proceedings and deported if they are convicted of certain crimes, including any "aggravated felony" under immigration law, crimes of moral turpitude, domestic violence offenses, or drug-related offenses. Even a misdemeanor conviction in state court can trigger deportation under federal immigration law. Additionally, lengthy absences from the U.S. or abandonment of U.S. residency can put your green card at risk. If you are a green card holder facing any criminal charge — no matter how minor it seems — it is essential that you speak with an attorney who understands both criminal and immigration law before accepting any plea deal.
